Masala Dosa

Masala dosa is a famous South Indian dish made with fermented rice batter and a spicy potato filling. It's crispy on the outside and soft inside, perfect for breakfast or lunch.

Ingredients:

  • Rice batter
  • Potatoes
  • Spices:
    • Mustard seeds
    • Curry leaves
    • Green chilies
  • Oil
  • Salt

Cooking Steps:

  1. Soak and grind rice and urad dal to make the batter.
  2. Ferment the batter overnight.
  3. Boil and mash potatoes.
  4. Heat oil and sauté mustard seeds, curry leaves, and green chilies.
  5. Mix the tempered spices with mashed potatoes.
  6. Heat a pan and spread a ladle of batter into a thin circle.
  7. Place some potato filling in the center.
  8. Fold the dosa and cook until golden brown.
  9. Serve hot with chutney and sambar.

Cooking Terms:

Sauté
Cooking quickly in a small amount of oil on high heat.
Garnish
Decorating the dish with herbs, spices, or other toppings before serving.

Paneer Butter Masala

Paneer Butter Masala is a creamy and rich North Indian curry made with cottage cheese (paneer), butter, cream, and tomato-based gravy, flavored with Indian spices.

Ingredients:

  • Paneer cubes
  • Tomatoes
  • Butter
  • Spices:
    • Garam masala
    • Red chili powder
    • Coriander powder
  • Fresh cream
  • Salt & sugar

Cooking Steps:

  1. Heat butter in a pan and sauté chopped onions.
  2. Add tomato puree and cook until oil separates.
  3. Add spices and a little sugar to balance flavors.
  4. Add paneer cubes and simmer for 5 minutes.
  5. Finish with cream and garnish with coriander leaves.
  6. Serve hot with naan or rice.

Cooking Terms:

Simmer
Cooking at a temperature just below boiling.
Puree
Blending ingredients into a smooth, thick paste.

Gulab Jamun

Gulab Jamun is a classic Indian dessert made from milk solids (khoya) and flour, deep-fried until golden and soaked in flavored sugar syrup.

Ingredients:

  • Khoya (milk solids)
  • Maida (refined flour)
  • Sugar Syrup:
    • Sugar
    • Water
    • Cardamom pods
    • Rose water
  • Ghee or oil for frying
  • Baking soda

Cooking Steps:

  1. Knead the khoya, maida, and baking soda into a soft dough.
  2. Roll into small smooth balls without cracks.
  3. Heat oil and fry the balls on low flame until golden brown.
  4. Make sugar syrup by boiling sugar, water, cardamom, and rose water.
  5. Soak the fried balls in warm syrup for at least 30 minutes.
  6. Serve warm or chilled as desired.

Cooking Terms:

Knead
Working dough with hands to make it smooth and elastic.
Syrup
Thick liquid made from sugar and water, often flavored.
