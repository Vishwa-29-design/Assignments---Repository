A mutual fund is a type of investment vehicle consisting of a portfolio of stocks, bonds, and other securities. Mutual funds in India are regulated by the SEBI (Securities and Exchange Board of India).[1] An Asset Management Company (AMC) is a company that manages the mutual fund.[2]

1 Mutual fund statistics

1.1 Assets Under Management (AUM)

The total AUM for all mutual funds in India was ₹ 37.33 lakh crore as of January 31, 2018. The data is released by the AMFI (Association of Mutual Funds in India).

1.2 Mutual fund categories based on Assets under management

Based on their assets under management (AUM), the top five mutual fund categories in India are:

  1. Equity-oriented schemes
  2. Debt-oriented schemes
  3. Hybrid schemes
  4. Solution-oriented schemes
  5. Other schemes

1.3 Top 10 Indian cities for mutual fund penetration

The cities with the highest penetration of mutual funds are:

2 Mutual fund category breakup

Mutual funds are primarily classified into three categories: equity, debt, and hybrid funds. The percentage of total AUM for each category as of a recent period is as follows:

  • Equity funds: 39%
  • Debt funds: 42%
  • Hybrid funds: 11%
  • Other schemes: 8%

3 Controversies

3.1 Franklin Templeton fiasco

In 2020, Franklin Templeton India decided to wind up six of its debt schemes, citing severe and dramatic drops in liquidity. The move locked in an estimated ₹ 26,000 crore of investor money, leading to widespread panic. The case was taken up by the Supreme Court of India.[3]

3.1.1 Subsequent events

Following a Supreme Court order, Franklin Templeton began the process of distributing the proceeds from the sale of assets to investors. The SEBI also imposed a fine on the AMC for alleged irregularities.[4]
